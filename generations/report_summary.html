High-Resolution Image Reconstruction with Latent Diffusion Models from Human Brain Activity

Summary of the paper

This paper presents a new method based on Latent Diffusion Models (LDMs) to reconstruct high-resolution images from human brain activity obtained via fMRI. The authors studied the inner mechanisms of the LDM by analyzing the components (latent vector of image Z, conditioning inputs C, and denoising U-Net) and their relationship to brain functions. The proposed method can reconstruct images with high fidelity without the need for additional training or fine-tuning of complex deep-learning models. Results show that the model can generate high-resolution images with high semantic fidelity, and that the latent representation of the image can be mapped to brain activity. Additionally, the paper explores the use of multiscale local image decoders, score matching, deep neural networks, and feature-space selection to improve image synthesis, capture the complexity of neural representations, and reconstruct complex images from brain activities.
